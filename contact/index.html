    Inquiry Guide

    Send enough context for a useful reply.

    The studio can respond faster when an inquiry identifies the artwork, intended use, location, deadline, and audience.

    Collectors

    Include the title, preferred dimensions, framing needs, delivery city, and whether the request is for home, office, or institutional display.

    Curators

    Include the exhibition theme, venue, dates, requested works, installation constraints, and any catalogue or public-program context.

    Editors and publishers

    Use the image licensing form to provide the image, media, territory, duration, audience, exclusivity, and deadline.

    Press and research

    Include the publication or institution, subject, deadline, requested interview format, and any artwork or historical record being checked.

    Print acquisition

    For collector inquiries, include title, preferred size, framing preference, and delivery city.

    Exhibition inquiry

    For exhibition proposals, include venue, dates, curatorial theme, and requested works.

    Licensing

    For publication or licensing, include image title, use, territory, duration, and deadline.

    Archive research

    For award, archive, or public page questions, include the image, competition, date range, or @rickykwokbc context you are researching.
