Series Statement

Motion is where the body leaves a trace of itself.

I treat sport as a language of line, pressure and repetition. Swimmers become bands of turquoise, divers form descending sequences and horses compress into a charging mass. I look for the instant when a recognizable action starts to dissolve into abstraction but still retains the emotional charge of competition or performance.

Blur is not a loss of information here; it is the subject. These photographs hold rhythm, strain and acceleration as visible material. The body remains present as a streak, a wake, a pulse or a curve through water and light.
