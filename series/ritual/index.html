Series Statement

Ritual begins where ordinary gestures become ceremonial.

I approach documentary photography as a form of theatre already present in daily life. Fishermen lift lamps on dark water; people gather around a hearth; smoke rises through old kitchens; elders and children share laughter outside village houses. The images are not staged, but I look for the moment when light and gesture briefly organize a scene for the camera.

Ritual is rooted in my attention to human rhythm: a hand extended toward fire, a face turned toward light, a small community held together by gesture. I want work, memory and performance to remain visible in the same frame.
