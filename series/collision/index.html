Series Statement

Collision treats time as physical material.

I use high-speed photography to turn fractions of a second into sculptural events. A droplet rises into a column, ash breaks into a blue-black cloud, a flower ruptures into dust, and an egg opens like a fragile vessel. Carefully prepared light, colour and timing meet the unpredictable behaviour of water, powder, smoke and impact.

These images sit between experiment and performance. I am interested in what form looks like before the eye has time to understand it—in making force visible as line, volume and atmosphere.
