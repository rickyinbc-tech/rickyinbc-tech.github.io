City Light

Hong Kong as theatre: dense, reflective, electric, and always in motion.

Collector InquiryAll Works

Series Statement

City Light begins with Hong Kong’s visual pressure.

I look at Hong Kong through its night surfaces: signboards, construction cranes, rain-slick roads, tramlines, street murals and the hard brightness of commercial light. I do not treat the city as a backdrop; I photograph it as an active force pressing on the body and the eye. In Light Encroached Homes, a low fisheye view turns Mong Kok into a chamber of signboards and darkness.

Across the series, architecture bends, traffic draws lines, crowds become atmosphere and night becomes a medium rather than an absence.
