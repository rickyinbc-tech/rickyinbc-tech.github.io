Photography Series

Four bodies of work, one practice of charged time.

Ricky Kwok’s fine-art photography moves between witnessed ritual, high-speed studio impact, sport abstraction, and Hong Kong’s electric night surfaces.

Primary Series

The portfolio is organized around four public bodies of work.

Each series page includes a short essay, selected images, print context, related awards, and links back into the wider archive.

Ritual

Human scenes where gesture, smoke, lamp light, and shared laughter carry the gravity of lived tradition.

Collision

Water, powder, ash, shell, and smoke treated as sculptural material inside fractions of a second.

Motion

Sport translated into abstraction, rhythm, blur, and the visual pressure of speed.

City Light

Hong Kong night as theatre: reflective, dense, electric, and always in motion.

Extended Themes

The series also gather into repeating visual questions.

Documentary Theatre

Fishermen, kitchens, terraces, workers, and village scenes appear as real encounters that already contain their own stage light.

Water Studies

Green Orb, Blue Column, Orange Suspension, Yellow Arc, and Magenta Collision isolate water as form, colour, and timing.

High-Speed Impact

Flowers, eggs, candles, ash, chalk, and powder are photographed at the instant they become fragments.

Hong Kong Night

Neon, light pollution, street processions, cranes, buses, and rain-slick roads define the local visual pressure of the work.

Project Archive

Horse Riding, Travel, Nepal Documentary, and Infrared Hong Kong add four legacy Behance project rooms to the official archive.
