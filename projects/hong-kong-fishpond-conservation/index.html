Project Context

A wetland subject documented through landscape and ecology.

This page follows the public competition record. It does not add an undocumented artist recollection or infer details about photographs that are not reproduced here.

The Hong Kong Bird Watching Society described the city's fishponds as both a distinctive landscape and a biologically rich wetland habitat. Its competition invited photographers to help more people see and understand that environment.

Fishpond Harvest records labour within that landscape: workers, waterproof clothing, nets and water share the frame. The official result places the photograph in the student landscape category.

Read the Hong Kong Bird Watching Society's competition record.

Present-day conservation context

Working fishponds connect habitat, heritage and livelihoods.

These sources provide current Hong Kong context; they do not establish the exact location or circumstances of any photograph on this page.

Wetland and cultural heritage

AFCD guidance on local wetland and fishpond conservation describes Deep Bay management agreements as supporting wetland ecology while preserving traditional fish farming as part of local cultural heritage.

Current programme

The Hong Kong Fishpond Conservation Scheme 2025–2027 combines habitat management, ecological surveys, community education and fisheries promotion in collaboration with fishpond operators.
