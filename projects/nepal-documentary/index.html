Nepal Documentary

Portraits and daily scenes from Nepal, built around direct presence, family, work, and street life.

Print / Licensing InquiryAll Projects

Project Notes

Nepal Documentary expands the official archive with a focused body of work.

A human documentary project: children, families, makers, workers, and elders photographed with warmth, dignity, and a strong sense of encounter.

The sequence is preserved as a room of its own so the rhythm between images remains legible, while the Selected Works page remains a tight exhibition edit.

The page also gives the Nepal archive a clearer search identity. Rather than treating the images as generic travel pictures, it frames them as documentary portraiture built around direct looking, daily work, street economy, family care, and the ethics of encounter.

Reading Guide

Portraiture, proximity, and public archive context.

This project supports the biography because it shows Kwok's documentary method: close enough to register individual presence, but organized carefully enough to read as a sequence.

Human Presence

The strongest images are built around direct faces, small gestures, and the visual dignity of everyday surroundings.

Daily Work

Kitchen work, street shaving, market labour, and road movement connect portraiture to social space.

Archive Role

The project gives the official site a source-backed room for Nepal work while keeping the main Selected Works page tightly edited.
