Photography Project / Hong Kong

Infrared Photography Hong Kong

Hong Kong garden and temple architecture reimagined through infrared colour, warm stone, and pale foliage.

View PhotographsAll Projects

Project Notes

Six photographs recast Hong Kong gardens, pavilions, and temple roofs through an infrared palette.

Trees become bronze and white, pavilions glow, and familiar garden architecture takes on a luminous, dreamlike register.

The sequence moves from enclosed paths and bridges to layered roofs and open sky. Warm stone, pale foliage, saturated colour, and small human figures hold the transformed scenes together.

  • Project: Infrared Photography Hong Kong
  • Focus: Infrared colour and Hong Kong architecture
  • Photographs: 6
  • First published: June 6, 2018 on Behance
