Project Notes

Travel: China Documentary Studies expands the official archive with a focused body of work.

This project brings together documentary photographs from China and surrounding travel work: river scenes, kitchens, fields, craftsmen, fishpond labour, and village encounters.

The sequence is preserved as a room of its own so the rhythm between images remains legible, while the Selected Works page remains a tight exhibition edit.

For SEO and archive clarity, this page names the project as travel documentary work rather than scattering the images across unrelated categories. It connects Fishpond Harvest, Coil Field, night fishermen, rural kitchens, and terraced landscapes back to one documentary thread.

Reading Guide

How this project fits the wider portfolio.

The travel archive is useful because it shows how Ricky Kwok moves between human presence, landscape structure, labour, and atmosphere before those concerns reappear in the edited fine-art series.

Documentary Theatre

Figures, tools, interiors, and landscapes are treated as active stages rather than neutral background information.

Labour and Gesture

Fishpond work, basket making, kitchen scenes, and field labour connect the project to the broader social-documentary side of the archive.

Landscape Context

River dusk, rice terraces, and misted distance explain why landscape in this portfolio is often read as mood, memory, and structure.
