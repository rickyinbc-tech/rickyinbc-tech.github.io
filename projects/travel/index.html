Photography Project / China

Travel: China Documentary Studies

A documentary sequence moving between labour, shared rituals, landscape, and rural life.

View PhotographsAll Projects

Project Notes

Eleven photographs connect labour, landscape, and shared space in a China-focused travel sequence.

The project brings together river scenes, kitchens, fields, craftspeople, fishpond labour, and village encounters. Fishpond Harvest, Coil Field, night fishermen, rural kitchens, and terraced landscapes form one documentary sequence.

Recurring structures hold the photographs together: a worker placed within a patterned field, fire or lamplight turning a task into a small stage, and water carrying both labour and stillness. Gesture, repetition, night light, and landscape move through the project without reducing its subjects to a single setting.

  • Project: Travel: China Documentary Studies
  • Focus: Documentary and travel photography
  • Photographs: 11
  • First published: September 3, 2015 on Behance

Project Threads

People, work, and landscape.

The sequence moves between close human presence and the larger environments in which work and daily life unfold.

Human Presence

Figures, tools, interiors, and landscapes meet in scenes shaped by attention to gesture and place.

Labour and Gesture

Fishpond work, basket making, kitchen scenes, and field labour connect repeated actions across the project.

Landscape

River dusk, rice terraces, and misted distance bring atmosphere and scale to the human scenes.
