Biography

Ricky Kwok 郭文棣, ARPS

A Hong Kong fine-art photographer working between documentary observation and constructed studio experiments.

ARPS: Associate of The Royal Photographic Society.

Artist Statement

I study the moment when ordinary reality becomes unstable.

A river lamp becomes a stage light, a swimmer dissolves into colour, an urban signboard becomes environmental pressure, and a droplet becomes sculpture.

Moving between documentary observation and constructed studio experiments, I treat time as physical material. The images are rooted in Hong Kong’s density, ritual life, and visual intensity, while extending into high-speed studies of water, ash, powder, and motion.

Bio

Hong Kong, light, ritual, and impact.

Ricky Kwok, also known as Kwok Man Tai and 郭文棣, is a Hong Kong fine-art and documentary photographer and an Associate of the Royal Photographic Society.

Practice

Kwok’s fine-art work moves through ritual scenes, Hong Kong urban night, sport abstraction, and controlled high-speed studies. The images often turn a brief instant into a visual object.

Training Record

Public records identify Kwok Man Tai as a CCC Chuen Yuen College student at the time of the 2014 HKU light pollution award, and later as an HKMU Cinematic, Design & Photographic Digital Art student in 2016.

Recognition

Selected verified records include Commended, Youth, Culture in the 2015 Sony World Photography Awards; the 2014 HKU Light Pollution Photography Competition champion; and student-category landscape and ecology wins in the Hong Kong Fishpond Conservation photography competition.

Distinction

ARPS means Associate of the Royal Photographic Society, an RPS distinction. It is not presented as a professional licence.
