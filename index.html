Artist Statement

Ricky Kwok photographs the instant when ordinary matter becomes theatre.

Known publicly as Ricky Kwok and Kwok Man Tai, the artist began photographing as a student and built an award-winning body of work across documentary, sport, abstract studio practice, and urban night photography. Public profiles record more than 100 awards in Hong Kong and overseas between 2013 and 2015.

His images move between human tenderness and precise physical force. A lantern on a river, a child mid-laugh, a swimmer dissolving into turquoise, a cigarette breaking apart in blue smoke: each frame treats time as a sculptural material.

Bodies of Work

Four visual languages, one eye for charged time.

The work moves fluidly between documentary witness and studio construction. The emotional center stays consistent: a decisive instant made unfamiliar, luminous, and alive.

Ritual

Human scenes where gesture, smoke, lamp light, and laughter carry the gravity of lived tradition.

Collision

Water, powder, ash, and shell are treated as sculptural material inside fractions of a second.

Motion

Sport is translated into abstraction, rhythm, blur, and the visual pressure of speed.

City Light

Hong Kong appears as theatre: dense, reflective, electric, and always in motion.
