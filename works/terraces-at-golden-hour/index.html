Terraces at Golden Hour studies landscape as a pattern made by labour. Water-filled rice terraces curve across the hills in repeated bands, catching warm light and turning the ground into a reflective drawing. The small figures walking through the scene keep the photograph from becoming pure abstraction. Their presence reveals scale, effort, and the human rhythm beneath the beauty of the land. Kwok’s composition holds the terraces as both image and system: a luminous surface shaped by generations of work. The photograph belongs to Ritual because it recognizes the quiet choreography of people moving through a constructed landscape. The gold light softens the scene, while layered depth reveals figures gradually within the repeated pattern.
