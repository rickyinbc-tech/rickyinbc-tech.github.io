Artwork Details

Ice Crown

  • Series: Collision
  • Photographer: Ricky Kwok / 郭文棣 / Kwok Man Tai
  • Artwork type: Photograph
  • Copyright: © Ricky Kwok. Written permission is required for reproduction.

Ice Crown turns a boxer’s portrait into an event of impact. Water bursts above the head like a temporary crown, while the gloves frame the lower part of the body with tension and readiness. The subject is athletic, but the image does not depend on a match or a ring. Instead, Kwok constructs a studio moment where force, performance, and portraiture meet. The boxer’s expression gives the photograph its human intensity; the water gives it form. In the Motion and Collision contexts, the image suggests that athletic energy can be photographed before or after the visible strike. The crown of water becomes both comic and heroic, a sculptural halo produced by collision. The work is direct, graphic, and physically immediate.

Caption: A portrait of athletic force crowned by frozen water.

Ice Crown connects athletic portraiture with Collision’s study of force and water as sculptural material.
