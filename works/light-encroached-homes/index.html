Light Encroached Homes / 光染民居

Illuminated signs and homes meet around the Mong Kok night sky.

Artwork Facts

Light Encroached Homes

  • Chinese title: 光染民居
  • Photographer: Ricky Kwok / Kwok Man Tai / 郭文棣
  • Year: 2014
  • Location: Mong Kok, Hong Kong
  • Artwork type: Photograph
  • Capture: Fisheye lens, ultra-low angle from street level, at about 10 p.m.

Documented Process

An ultra-low view of Mong Kok at night.

This first-person historical account is adapted from Ricky Kwok's statement published by The University of Hong Kong in 2014.

At about 10 p.m., I lay on my back on a Mong Kok street and photographed upward with a fisheye lens. From ground level, the district's signs rose around the sky.

Entering the competition led me to look more carefully at Hong Kong's light-pollution conditions. As I walked and observed, I noticed how many advertising signs stood close to homes and began thinking about residents trying to sleep while exposed to that light.

Read the complete Hong Kong light-pollution project record.

Verified Context

Institutional result and public interpretation.

The University of Hong Kong's May 29, 2014 record supplies the result, production account, judge's reading and exhibition announcement.

Result

HKU identifies 郭文棣 as champion for 光染民居 in a field of 154 photography entries.

Judge's reading

Judge Lau Chi-fung interpreted the image as communicating how intense advertising light near homes could prevent sleep and as underscoring the urgency of regulating light pollution and light nuisance.

Exhibition announcement

HKU announced a free presentation of outstanding competition works in the Hong Kong Space Museum lobby from July 9 to August 11, 2014.
