Blue Hour, River Stage is built around a quiet theatrical balance. Bamboo rafts rest on still water, cormorants hold the foreground, and lamps create points of warmth inside the blue evening. The raft reads as a stage, the water as a reflective floor, and the birds as witnesses. Any suggestion of a “disappearing rhythm” is an artistic interpretation, not a documented claim about cultural or economic change; exact place, period, activity, and field context must be confirmed before editorial use.
