Short biography

Ricky Kwok (Kwok Man Tai / 郭文棣), ARPS, is a Hong Kong fine-art and documentary photographer. His work moves between observed public life and constructed studio experiments, focusing on urban light, ritual, athletic motion, water, and impact. Selected recognition includes Commended, Youth, Culture at the 2015 Sony World Photography Awards; HKU’s 2014 Light Pollution Photography Competition; the Hong Kong Bird Watching Society’s 2014 fishpond competition; and the 2013 「城市印象＠你我社區」Photography Competition.

Extended biography

Ricky Kwok, also known as Kwok Man Tai and 郭文棣, is a Hong Kong fine-art and documentary photographer and an Associate of the Royal Photographic Society. His practice moves between observed public life and constructed studio experiments, using timing, gesture, light, and physical force as visual material. Four recurring bodies of work—Ritual, Collision, Motion, and City Light—range from fishermen, festivals, and urban night to high-speed studies of water, powder, ash, and athletic movement. Selected results include Commended, Youth, Culture at the 2015 Sony World Photography Awards; Champion of HKU’s 2014 Light Pollution Photography Competition for Light Encroached Homes / 光染民居; four student-category landscape and ecology placements in the 2014 Hong Kong Bird Watching Society fishpond competition; Outstanding Award in the Secondary School Section of the 2013 「城市印象＠你我社區」Photography Competition; and Merit Prize in the 2016 Equal Opportunities Photo Competition. National Geographic Channel’s 2014 Nat Geo Awards result for 整整有條 is retained as a contemporaneous-report record. See the source-linked recognition record for exact wording and citations.
