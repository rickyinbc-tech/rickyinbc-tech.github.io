Studio Standards

Know the object before you acquire it.

Every acquisition is governed by a work-specific written offer. It identifies the photograph, the physical object, the edition or inventory status, the price, and the practical terms before any commitment is requested.

Written Offer

Six groups of facts must be confirmed.

The public portfolio is an artwork record, not a checkout page. Commercial and production facts become authoritative only when the studio supplies them for the named work.

Identity

Artwork title, artist, series or project, image reference, orientation and the exact work being discussed.

Edition & inventory

Whether the work is editioned, the edition and artist-proof structure if applicable, permitted sizes, available number, and any prior production.

Physical object

Print process, paper or substrate, image and sheet dimensions, borders, finishing, mounting, signature, numbering and any framing specification.

Documentation

The invoice, receipt and any certificate or accompanying record that will be supplied. A certificate is never assumed unless the offer confirms it.

Price & production

Currency, price, payment stages, taxes where known, production time, proof approval where relevant, and the offer’s validity period.

Delivery & care

Shipping origin, destination eligibility, packaging, insurance, duties, delivery estimate, damage procedure, installation and care guidance.

Authority

One record, different levels of access.

The photograph stays visually primary. Evidence, rights and transaction details remain available without competing with it.

  • Artwork page: the permanent public identity, context, rights notice and inquiry pathways.
  • Project or source page: verified institutional, exhibition or research evidence where available.
  • Written offer: the work-specific acquisition terms on which a collector may rely.
  • Invoice and documentation: the final transaction and object records supplied after agreement.

No Unstated Claims

Unknown facts remain unknown until checked.

The site does not infer a print process, edition size, certificate, frame, price or availability from an image file or an earlier exhibition record.

Collector requests are checked against the artist’s current records. If a term cannot be verified, it is not presented as part of the offer. Image licensing is a separate permission pathway and does not transfer ownership of copyright.
